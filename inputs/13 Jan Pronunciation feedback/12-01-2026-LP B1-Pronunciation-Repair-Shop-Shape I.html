The Pronunciation Soundcheck

Objective: By the end of the lesson, learners will be better able to use connected speech (linking) and sentence stress to sound more natural and fluent in the context of discussing their future hopes.

Date: 12-01-2026

Systems/Skills: Pronunciation / Speaking

CEFR Level: B1 (Mixed A2-B2)

Teacher: Ed Rush

Duration: 46 minutes

Materials: Individual Pronunciation Feedback Reports, Pronunciation Soundcheck Worksheet (Typst V1)

Assessment: Peer-reviewed speaking task using native-level pronunciation checklists.

DIFFERENTIATION

This lesson plan is built on the core principles of differentiation. Unlike traditional "one-size-fits-all" feedback, each student works from an individual feedback report detailing their unique pronunciation profile. Furthermore, the final output is differentiated by level: students select a speaking task (A2, B1, or B2) that aligns with their current linguistic proficiency, ensuring every learner is challenged at exactly the right level.

STAGE TWO: THE HERO TOOL (PRECISION FIXES)
3 To introduce the "Hero Tools" for clarity Mixing the Track
  • The Anchor (Final Consonants): T demonstrates the 'dropped consonant' failure. "En-gineer" vs "EngineeR". The final sound anchors the word.
  • The Fade-Out (Tapered Endings): T addresses shouting final syllables ('future-AH'). Use the 'fader' hand gesture to show a soft release.
  • The Crossfader (Linking): Introduce ‿ as the smooth transition between tracks.
  • The Bass Kick (Stress): Content words get the beat.
  • Drill marker sentences focusing on these 'Studio Tools'.
 10 T-Ss
STAGE THREE: THE LIVE PERFORMANCE
4 To prepare for production with scaffolding Selecting the Path
  • Ss choose their task level (A2, B1, or B2) on the worksheet.
  • Ss write their one-minute script for "My Hope for the Future".
  • Blueprint Stage: Ss must underline stressed words and draw linkages (‿) on their own script before speaking.
 10 Indiv
5 To practice and peer-review The Quality Check
  • Pairs: Ss perform their one-minute task.
  • Partner uses the Peer Review Checklist at the bottom of the worksheet.
  • Ss swap roles and repeat.

Answer Key / Model Structures

This lesson is production-focused. Teachers and students should look for:

  • A2: Clear /s/ /t/ /d/ endings.
  • B1: Natural rhythm (not robot-like).
  • B2: Complex linking (vowel-to-vowel, e.g., "be‿an").
