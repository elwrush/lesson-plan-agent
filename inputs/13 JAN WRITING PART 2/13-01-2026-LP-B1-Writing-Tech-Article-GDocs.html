B1 Writing: The 20/20 Tech Article

Objective: By the end of the lesson, learners will be better able to write a high-scoring PET article about technology, using a clear paragraph structure (Introduction, Body Paragraph, Conclusion) and complex clauses and modals to answer all parts of the question.

Date: 13-01-26

Focus: Writing (Productive Skills)

CEFR Level: B1

Teacher: Ed Rush

Duration: 46 minutes

Materials: 4-Page Worksheet, Video: BBC Dad Office Interruption (YouTube)

Assessment: Writing CA (PET Article Task 2)


Lesson Stages (Shape F - Process Writing)

Stage Aim Procedure Time Int.
STAGE ONE: Lead-in & Model Analysis
1 To activate interest and analyze a high-scoring model Lead-in to the Topic
  • Show Video: BBC Dad Office Interruption (see materials).
  • Ask: "Why is this funny?" (Contrast between professionalism and chaos, child in walker, etc.)
  • Hand out the Worksheet. Analyze Task 1 (Reference).
  • Guided Analysis: Locate answers to the 3 prompt questions in the model.
 8 T-S
2 To identify useful language patterns Language Analysis
  • Focus on the 3 core patterns: Superlatives, Specific Details (such as...), and Balanced Opinion (modals).
 5 T-S
STAGE TWO: Preparation (Planning)
3 To plan ideas for the main body paragraph The Layout Plan
  • Direct attention to Task 2 (Planning).
  • Ss complete the drafting grid (Topic Sentence / Evidence).
  • Teacher monitors for logical flow.
 8 S
STAGE THREE: Writing Task (Drafting)
4 To write the response using the target language Writing Phase
  • Ss complete Task 3 (Final Build).
  • Draft full article (100 words).
  • Requirement: Include the 3 target phrases.
 15 S
STAGE FOUR: Review & Feedback
5 To check for task achievement The Logic Check
  • Peer Check: Swap Worksheets.
  • Verify: Identified the app? Specific example? Balanced opinion?
 5 S-S
6 To consolidate learning Final Feedback
  • Showcase 2-3 brilliant examples from the class.
  • Address 1 common error on the board.
 5 T-S

Total Time: 46 minutes


Target Language Key

1. Expressing Preference: "The app I use most frequently is..."

2. Detailed Complex Clause: "In my view, computers are essential because..."

3. Adding Detail: "Furthermore, they allow us to [Positive Result]."
