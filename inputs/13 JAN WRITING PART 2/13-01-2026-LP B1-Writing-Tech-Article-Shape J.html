B1 Writing: The 20/20 Tech Article

Objective: By the end of the lesson, learners will be better able to write a high-scoring PET article about technology, using a clear paragraph structure (Introduction, Body Paragraph, Conclusion) and complex clauses and modals to answer all parts of the question.

Date: 13-01-26

Focus: Writing (Productive Skills)

CEFR Level: B1

Teacher: Ed Rush

Duration: 46 minutes

Materials: 4-Page Worksheet (Model Analysis & Writing Task), Video: BBC Dad Office Interruption (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Mh4f9AYRCZY)

Assessment: Writing CA (PET Article Task 2)

Lesson Stages (Shape F - Process Writing)

Stage Aim Procedure Time Interaction
STAGE ONE: Lead-in & Model Analysis
1 To activate interest and analyze a high-scoring model Lead-in to the Topic
  • Show Video: BBC Dad Office Interruption (see materials).
  • Ask: "Why is this funny?" Elicit ideas (Contrast between professional/chaos, the mother's reaction, the baby in the walker).
  • Hand out the Worksheet. Open to Task 1 (Reference).
  • Read the "Laughter" prompt (Q1-Q3).
  • Read the Model Answer (Candidate C).
  • Guided Analysis: Ask Ss to find WHERE the writer answers each question.
    • Q1 (What is it?): "The comedy I love the most is..."
    • Q2 (Why?): "Actors must overcome quests..."
    • Q3 (Importance?): "Laughing helps release stress..."
 8 T-Ss
2 To identify useful language patterns Language Analysis
  • Highlight the 3 key phrases used in the model:
  • 1. Superlative Choice: "The [Show] I love the most is..."
  • 2. Specific Detail: "They must overcome challenges, such as..."
  • 3. Balanced View: "It allows us to [X], but it may also [Y]."
 5 T-Ss
STAGE TWO: Preparation (Planning)
3 To plan ideas for the main body paragraph The Layout Plan
  • Direct attention to Task 2 (Planning) in the Worksheet.
  • Explain the task: "Write about a technology you use."
  • Ss complete the grid:
    • Topic Sentence: e.g., "I believe that computers are essential..."
    – Supporting Details: e.g., "Furthermore, it allows students to..."
  • T monitors and checks for logical connections.
 8 S
STAGE THREE: Writing Task (Drafting)
4 To write the response using the target language Writing Phase
  • Ss go to Task 3 (Final Build).
  • Write the full article (approx. 100 words).
  • Requirement: Must include the 3 target phrases from Stage 2.
  • T monitors, correcting individual errors on the spot.
 15 S
STAGE FOUR: Review & Feedback
5 To check for task achievement The Logic Check
  • Peer Check: Swap Worksheets.
  • Checklist:
    • Did they name the specific app/website? (Q1)
    • Did they give a specific example of why? (Q2)
    • Did they give a balanced opinion? (Q3)
 5 Ss-Ss
6 To consolidate learning Final Feedback
  • T highlights 2-3 good examples of "Proof" from the class.
  • T corrects 1 common error on the board.
 5 T-Ss

Total Time: 46 minutes

Target Language Key

1. Expressing Preference: "The app I use most frequently is..."

2. Detailed Complex Clause: "In my view, computers are essential because..."

3. Adding Detail: "Furthermore, they allow us to [Positive Result]."
