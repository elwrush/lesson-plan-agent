B1 Writing: The 20/20 Tech Article

Objective: By the end of the lesson, learners will be better able to write a high-scoring PET article about technology, using the SCR structure to organize their response and varied lexis to engage the reader.

Date: 13-01-26

Focus: Writing (Productive Skills)

CEFR Level: B1

Teacher: Ed Rush

Duration: 46 minutes

Materials: Cambridge "Laughter" Model Answer, Tech Article Writing Task, Dot-Dash Worksheet

Assessment: PET Writing Part 2 (Article) - Technology Topic

Lesson Stages

Stage Aim Procedure Time Int.
STAGE ONE: The "Spaghetti Code" Problem
1 To show weak writing lacking structure The Glitch
  • Show a "Bad Code" article about Tech:
    "I use AI for homework. It is fast. It helps me. Sometimes it is wrong. But I like it."
  • Ask: "What score is this?" (Probably 3/5).
  • Ask: "Why?" (It functions, but it's amateur. No sophisticated logic).
  • Metaphor: "This is Spaghetti Code. It works, but it's unstructured and hard to read. We want 'Clean Architecture'."
 5 T-Ss
STAGE TWO: Reverse Engineering the 20/20 Model
2 To analyze the "Source Code" of a perfect answer Decompiling Candidate C
  • Hand out the Cambridge "Laughter" model (Candidate C).
  • "This scored 20/20. Let's see how it's built."
  • Highlight the SCR Structure:
    • Situation (Intro): "I would like to deal with..." -> "I love to watch comedies..."
    • Complication (The Details): "The Challenge" -> "Famous actors must overcome challenging quests..."
    • Resolution (Conclusion): "The Benefit" -> "Laughing can help us release stress..."
 8 T-Ss
3 To extract key lexis (The "Upgrades") Extracting the Code Snippets
  • Identify the vocabulary that upgrades the score:
  • Upgrade 1: "The comedy I love the most is..." (Emphasis)
  • Upgrade 2: "Must overcome challenging quests such as..." (Complex sentence)
  • Upgrade 3: "It may also make us..." (Modals for speculation)
  • "We will COPY this code for our Tech article."
 5 T-Ss
STAGE THREE: Planning the New Build (Dot-Dash)
4 To plan the Tech article using Dot-Dash System Architecture
  • Topic: "Technology: Helpful or Harmful?"
  • Demonstrate Dot-Dash for Paragraph 2 (Complication/Challenge):
    DOT: Generative AI is powerful but risky.
    DASH 1: It writes essays in seconds.
    DASH 2: It hallucinates facts that aren't true. (The "Review" detail)
  • Ss complete Dot-Dash plan for their chosen tech (Algorithms, VR, AI).
 8 S
STAGE FOUR: Writing the Code
5 To compile the final text Drafting Paragraph 2 (The Core)
  • "Write your Paragraph 2. Use the 'Code Snippet':"
  • "In [App/Tech], users must overcome complex issues, such as..."
  • Example: "In social media, users must overcome algorithmic addiction, such as scrolling for hours without realizing."
  • Ss write. T monitors for "Spaghetti Code" (rambling).
 10 S
6 To complete the full program Full Compile
  • Ss write Paragraph 1 (Situation) and Paragraph 3 (Resolution).
  • P3 Requirement: Use the "Wider View" snippet:
    "Technology can help us to [connect], but it may also make us [lazy] too!"
 5 S
STAGE FIVE: Debugging
7 To peer-review and check against 20/20 specs Code Review
  • Peer swap. Check for bugs:
  • ☐ Is there a specific example (e.g., specific game name)?
  • ☐ Did they use "Must overcome such as..."?
  • ☐ Is the conclusion balanced?
 5 Ss-Ss

Total Time: 46 minutes

Model "code" Snippets

Snippet 1 (Structure): "The [Tech] I love the most is..."

Snippet 2 (Deep Detail): "Users must overcome complex challenges, such as [Example A] and [Example B]."

Snippet 3 (Wider View): "It allows us to [positive], but it may also make us [negative] too!"
