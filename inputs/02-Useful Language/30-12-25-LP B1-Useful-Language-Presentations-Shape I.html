Useful Language for Presentations

Objective: By the end of the lesson, learners will be better able to use signposting language and functional phrases in the context of presenting a book or story they have read.

Date: 30-12-25

Systems/Skills: Systems (Functional Language)

CEFR Level: B1

Teacher: Ed Rush

Duration: 46 minutes

Materials: useful-language-worksheet.pdf (Functional language phrases categorized by presentation stages: Hook, Exposition, Opinion, Closing).

Assessment: n/a

Lesson Stages

Stage Aim Procedure Time Interaction
STAGE ONE: SITUATION - Discussing Favorite Stories
1 To establish context and build interest Favorite Stories Warm-up
  • Display images of popular books/movies (Harry Potter, Hunger Games, etc.).
  • Ss discuss in pairs: "What is the best story you've ever read/watched? Why?"
  • Teacher elicits a few answers.
 5 T-Ss / Ss-Ss
2 To assess current ability The Mini-Presentation Try-out
  • "Tell your partner about your story. You have 1 minute. Go!"
  • Teacher monitors, listening for structure (or lack thereof).
 4 Ss-Ss
STAGE TWO: COMPLICATION - The Communication Breakdown
3 To highlight functional failure The Messy Transcript
  • Show a transcript of a "Chaotic Presentation": "I read Harry Potter. He lives in a house. He goes to school. I like it. He has magic. Scholastic published it. The end."
  • Detective Work: Ask Ss: "How does this feel to listen to? Is it exciting? Is it easy to follow?" (Ss suggest: Boring, confusing, robotic).
  • Reveal the cost: "The audience gets lost. Your message is like a car with no map—you're driving but no one knows where you're going!"
 7 T-Ss
4 To address Thai L1 interference The "Lost" Audience Scaffolding
  • Explain: In Thai, sentences are often connected by context alone, but in English presentations, we need signposting words to guide the listener.
  • Show why "The reason is..." (เหตุผลคือ...) is better than just starting a new sentence without a link (e.g., "I like it. It because...").
 5 T-Ss
STAGE THREE: RESOLUTION - The Hero Tools (Signposts)
5 To introduce the "Hero Tool" Presentation Signposts are your GPS
  • Distribute the Useful Language Worksheet.
  • Introduce the sections (Hook, Exposition, Opinion, Closing) as "Stages of the Map".
  • Metaphor: These phrases are the GPS coordinates for your audience. They tell the audience: "Now we are starting," "Now there is a problem," "Now I am finished."
6 T-Ss
6 To practice target language form and use Mix & Match / Pronunciation Drill
  • Drill the "Hooks": "Have you ever wondered...?" (Emphasize tone/intonation).
  • Practice the "Connecting Phrases" from the Bonus section: "However...", "In addition...".
  • Show how "Although..." (แม้ว่า...) connects ideas more formally than a simple "But".
 6 T-Ss
STAGE FOUR: RESOLUTION - Productive Practice
7 To apply language in a structured way Drafting the Hero Presentation
  • Ss work individually to draft a brief summary of their favorite story using at least one phrase from each worksheet stage.
  • Teacher monitors, ensuring "Hooks" and "Exposition" markers are used correctly.
 8 S / T-S
8 To perform and provide feedback The "Pro" Presentation Sweep
  • Ss present in small groups (3-4).
  • Audience has a "Signpost Checklist": Put a tick when you hear a transition phrase.
  • Feedback: "Which presentation was easiest to follow? Why?"
 5 Ss-Ss

Teacher Notes & Thai Scaffolding

Thai L1 Scaffolds for B1 Presentation Language:

  • The "Because" Bridge: Thai students often say "I like it. It is exciting." Encourage "I like it because it's exciting" (เพราะว่า) to sound more mature.
  • Signposting (GPS): Explain that phrases like "First of all" or "Finally" act like mapping points for content flow. Without them, the listener doesn't know where one point ends and the next begins.
  • Recommendation Form: "I would definitely recommend..." sounds much more professional than "I think you read it." Explain that "would" (น่าจะ/ควรจะ) adds a level of politeness and expertise.
