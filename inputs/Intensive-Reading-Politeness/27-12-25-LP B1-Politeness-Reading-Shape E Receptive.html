What does polite mean to you?

Objective: By the end of the lesson, learners will have practiced and developed the sub-skills of reading for gist, specific information, and detail in the context of an article about politeness across cultures and generations.

Date: 27-12-25

Skills: Reading

CEFR Level: B1

Teacher: Ed Rush

Duration: 46 minutes

Materials: Worksheet on comprehending a reading and developing ideas about what it means to be polite in a given culture. Includes Entry Ticket (PET Part 2 matching task), reading text with cross-cultural image, vocabulary exercises, and discussion questions.

Assessment: n/a

Lesson Stages

Stage Aim Procedure Time Interaction
Lead-in
1 To lead-in, activate schema, and pre-teach vocabulary Part 1 – Thai Wai Discussion + Video
  • Display an image of a Thai person doing the wai with the numeral "3" next to it.
  • In pairs, Ss discuss what the three levels of the Thai wai are and what they mean. 2 min. Feedback.
  • Play YouTube video: https://www.youtube.com/shorts/jG9hxz9fZ0Y to check. Brief discussion.
  • Transition: "Today we'll read about how politeness varies across cultures and generations."

Part 2 – Entry Ticket: Tools of the Trade
  • Ss match 5 tools to 5 people based on professions (PET Part 2 format). 3 min.
  • Pair check. 1 min. Whole-class feedback.

Part 3 – Pre-teach Vocabulary
  • Present 5 vocabulary items: behavior, interrupt, appropriate, offend, acceptable
  • For each: word /phonemic/: Thai translation + context sentences in English and Thai
  • Drill pronunciation chorally and individually.
 14 T-Ss
Ss-Ss
Reading for gist, detail, and specific information
2 To practice reading for gist, detail, and specific information Section A – Vocabulary preview
  • Ss complete sentences with words from the box. 4 min.
  • Pair check. Whole-class feedback.

Section B – Rating behaviors
  • Ss rate 4 behaviors on a scale (10 = very rude, 1 = it's fine). 2 min.
  • In pairs, compare ratings and discuss differences. 2 min. Feedback.

Section C – Global reading (Topic sentences)
  • Explain topic sentences. Ss read quickly and underline topic sentences in paragraphs 2-4. 3 min.
  • Ss match main ideas A, B, C with paragraphs 2-4. 2 min.
  • Pair check. Whole-class feedback.

Section D – Close reading (Gap fill)
  • Ss read again and complete the summary paragraph with 6 missing pieces of information. 5 min.
  • Pair check. 2 min. Whole-class feedback.
  • Highlight contrast between older and younger generations' views on politeness.
 22 T-Ss
Ss-Ss
Post-reading speaking task
3 To develop critical thinking and personalize the topic Section E – Critical thinking discussion
  • Display discussion questions: 1) What behavior do you find rude? 2) Are younger people less polite than older people?
  • Model useful language: "I really dislike it when...", "I do / don't think so because..."
  • In pairs, Ss discuss both questions. 5 min.
  • Rearrange pairs. Ss report what their previous partner said. 2 min.
  • Whole-class feedback. Content + language feedback.
  • Closing: "Politeness is cultural – what's polite in one culture may not be in another."
 10 T-Ss
Ss-Ss
