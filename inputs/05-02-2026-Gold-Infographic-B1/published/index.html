GOLD: FACT OR FICTION?

Gold is edible.
All gold ever mined fits in 2 Olympic pools.
There is gold in your body.
Quick warmer. Students guess. Reveal the answers to spark interest.
QUIZ: ANSWER 1/3

Gold is edible.

TRUE - FACT
Gold leaf is used in luxury desserts and drinks. It's safe to eat but has no taste.

Edible gold (gold leaf or gold flakes) is classified as food-safe. It's often used to decorate chocolates, cakes, and fancy drinks. Remember: just because it's edible doesn't mean it's tasty!
Gold is actually used in high-end gastronomy around the world.
QUIZ: ANSWER 2/3

All gold ever mined fits in 2 Olympic pools.

FALSE - FICTION
If you gathered all the gold ever mined, it would fill about 3-4 Olympic swimming pools.

This common myth is close but not quite right! While gold is very dense (heavy), the total amount mined throughout human history is larger than most people expect.
This shows how valuable gold is - we've been mining it for thousands of years!
QUIZ: ANSWER 3/3

There is gold in your body.

TRUE - FACT
The human body contains approximately 0.2 milligrams of gold, mostly in the blood.

Amazingly, trace amounts of gold exist naturally in our bodies! This tiny amount comes from the foods we eat and the environment. It's not enough to extract, but it's there!
This is one of the most surprising facts for students!

SCAN: ANSWER 1/5

Where did the gold on Earth's surface come from?

Asteroids and Meteorites
[Para 1] "Gold was delivered to Earth by asteroids and meteorites in a period called the Late Heavy Bombardment, about 4 billion years ago."

Before asteroids brought gold, Earth's surface had very little. This 'golden rain' happened during a period when space rocks constantly hit our planet!
This explains why gold is so rare on Earth's surface today.
SCAN: ANSWER 2/5

How was gold formed in the universe?

Supernova Explosions
[Para 2] "Gold is created in the most powerful explosions in the universe: supernovae and neutron star collisions."

Gold is literally STARDUST! It was forged in the extreme heat and pressure of dying stars exploding. Every gold atom in your jewelry once belonged to a star!
This connects to the ORIGIN vocabulary word from earlier.
SCAN: ANSWER 3/5

Where is most of the gold on Earth?

At Earth's Core (99%)
[Para 3] "Approximately 99% of all the gold on Earth is located in its core, thousands of kilometers below the surface."

We can never access this gold! It's too deep and the pressure is too extreme. The gold we find is just the tiny fraction that stayed near the surface.
This is why gold mining is so difficult and expensive.

TASK: EXERCISE 1 (PART 2)

Scanning task Part 2. Focus on formation and uses.
SCAN: ANSWER 4/5

How are gold deposits formed underground?

Hot Water Through Rock
[Para 4] "Gold deposits are formed when hot water circulates through rock, depositing gold in cracks and veins."

This is called 'hydrothermal deposition.' Hot water dissolves tiny amounts of gold from rock, then cools down and leaves the gold behind in cracks!
This explains why gold is often found near hot springs or volcanic areas.
SCAN: ANSWER 5/5

What are the five main uses of gold?

Jewelry, Technology, Investment, Medicine, Dentistry
[Para 5] "Gold is used in: Jewelry, Technology (electronics), Investment (bars/coins), Medicine, and Dentistry."

Gold's unique properties (doesn't rust, conducts electricity, is biocompatible) make it valuable for ALL these industries - not just jewelry!
Technology use is growing rapidly due to gold's excellent conductivity.
