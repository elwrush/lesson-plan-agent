Social Media Society

Objective: By the end of the lesson, learners will be better able to skim reading texts to identify tone (positive, negative, or balanced) in the context of social media's impact on society.

Date: 06-01-26

Systems/Skills: Reading (Receptive Skills)

CEFR Level: B1

Teacher: Ed Rush

Duration: 46 minutes

Materials: Intensive Reading worksheet featuring 8 perspectives on social media with tone analysis task and "Skimming for Tone" strategy guide.

Assessment: n/a

Lesson Stages

Stage Aim Procedure Time Interaction
STAGE ONE: SITUATION - Social Media is Everywhere
1 To establish common ground The Daily Check Poll
  • Display poll question: "How many social media apps do you check before breakfast?"
  • Ss raise hands for: 0 / 1-2 / 3-5 / 5+ ("I am the internet")
  • Brief discussion: "Why? What are you checking for?" Create sense that social media is central to daily life.
 3 T-Ss
2 To pre-teach blocking vocabulary Essential Vocabulary (Thai scaffolds)
  • antisocial /ˌæntiˈsəʊʃəl/: ไม่ชอบสังคม
    Some teenagers become antisocial and prefer staying in their rooms.
    วัยรุ่นบางคนไม่ชอบสังคมและชอบอยู่ในห้องคนเดียว
  • hikikomori /ˌhɪkiˈkɒməri/: คนที่หลบหนีสังคม (ภาษาญี่ปุ่น)
    The term hikikomori describes extreme social withdrawal.
    คำว่าฮิกิโกะโมริหมายถึงการถอนตัวจากสังคมอย่างรุนแรง
  • popularity contest /ˌpɒpjuˈlærəti ˈkɒntest/: การแข่งขันเรื่องความนิยม
    School shouldn't be a popularity contest.
    โรงเรียนไม่ควรเป็นการแข่งขันเรื่องความนิยม
Drill chorally. Keep it brief. 		5 T-Ss
STAGE TWO: COMPLICATION - Too Many Opinions!
3 To trigger the functional breakdown The Challenge Revealed
  • Project or distribute worksheet showing the 8 text sections.
  • Ask: "Is social media good or bad? What does this text say?"
  • Ss attempt to skim but get overwhelmed: "There are 8 different perspectives! Which ones are positive? Which are negative?"
  • KEY INSIGHT: "Your habit of trying to understand EVERY word is making you confused. You're running out of time!"
 4 T-Ss
4 To deepen the problem Pattern Recognition
  • "Let's test something. Look at Section 1. Can you tell if it's positive or negative?"
  • Ss struggle: "It talks about changes... is that good or bad?"
  • "What about Section 2? Section 5?"
  • Ss realize: They can't decide WITHOUT understanding every word.
  • "But what if you don't have time to translate everything? How do you decide quickly?"
 4 T-Ss
STAGE THREE: RESOLUTION - Skimming for Tone is Your Hero Tool
5 To introduce the skill as a solution The Hero Tool: Skimming for Tone
  • Present the strategy from the worksheet's orange box:
  • Don't read everything! Use the First Sentence and Linking Words to find the tone.
  • Show examples:
    • Positive (+): connect, help, opportunity, socialize
    • Negative (-): antisocial, anxious, depressed, unfortunately
    • Balanced (=): "on the one hand... on the other hand"
  • Demonstration: "Section 2 - first sentence says 'antisocial' and 'shut themselves away.' That's negative! I didn't need to translate 'hikikomori'!"
 6 T-Ss
6 To apply the Hero Tool to realistic scenarios Detective Work - Find the Tone
  • Ss work in pairs to analyze all 8 sections using the table on the worksheet.
  • For each section:
    • Decide: Positive (+) / Negative (-) / Balanced (=)
    • Underline ONE keyword or phrase as evidence
  • Monitor and guide: "Did you find a tone word? Did you check the first sentence?"
  • Encourage speed: "You don't need perfect understanding. Find the clue and move on!"
 18 Ss-Ss
7 To consolidate learning through feedback Whole-Class Feedback
  • Review answers together (use Answer Key).
  • Ask: "Which words helped you decide? Did anyone ignore difficult words successfully?"
  • Highlight successful skimming examples: "Great! You found 'connect' in Section 3 and knew it was positive WITHOUT translating everything."
 4 T-Ss
8 To reflect on the transformation Reflection & Discussion
  • "Before today, what did you do when you saw a long English text?"
  • Ss reflect: "Tried to translate every word / gave up / got stressed"
  • "Now, what will you do?"
  • Key takeaway: "You don't need every word. You need the RIGHT words."
  • Extension (if time): Discuss worksheet question - "Do you agree we should 'take a break' from social media?"
 2 T-Ss

Answer Key

Tone Analysis Task

Section Tone Evidence (Acceptable Keywords)
1. Changing everything Balanced (=) "positive or negative development?" (question shows both sides)
2. Less connected Negative (-) "antisocial", "shut themselves away", "hikikomori"
3. Only way to socialize Positive (+) "socialize", "stay in touch", "feel part of a wider group"
4. Not just for young people Positive (+) "keep in touch", "growing groups"
5. Online profile Negative (-) "anxious", "depressed", "compare ourselves"
6. Being popular Negative (-) "popularity contest", "anxious", "feel hurt"
7. Wanting everything now Negative (-) "unfortunately", "worse at waiting", "issue worse"
8. Time to stop? Balanced (=) "On the one hand... On the other hand", "connected... anxious"
