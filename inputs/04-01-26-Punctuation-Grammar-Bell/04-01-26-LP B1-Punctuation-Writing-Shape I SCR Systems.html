Punctuation & Capitalisation Masterclass

Objective: By the end of the lesson, learners will be better able to apply the mechanical rules of spacing, capitalisation, and sentence boundaries to produce a clear, coherent written recommendation.

Date: 04-01-26

Systems/Skills: Writing Systems (Punctuation)

CEFR Level: B1

Teacher: Ed Rush

Duration: 46 minutes

Materials: Worksheet 'Punctuation & Capitalisation Masterclass' (04-01-26-Punctuation-Grammar-Bell.html)

Assessment: Writing Task (Email Recommendation)

Lesson Stages

Stage Aim Procedure Time Interaction
STAGE ONE: Situation - Awareness of the Rules
1 To establish correct spacing habits (Situation) Lead-in: The Space Invaders
  • T writes "I love Thai food . It is spicy ." on board.
  • Ask Ss: "Does this look strange?" Elicit the "floating dot" space issue.
  • Ss complete Task 1: Circle Correct/Incorrect.
  • Feedback: Emphasize the rule "Punctuation sticks to the word before it".
 5 T-Ss
2 To review capitalisation rules (Situation) Captain Capital
  • Ss look at Task 2 rule box. Elicit examples for each bullet point (e.g., Country = Thailand, Brand = Nike).
  • Ss work in pairs to find the 8 errors in the text.
  • Feedback: Ss come to board to circle errors on projection or projected text.
 8 Ss-Ss
STAGE TWO: Complication - The Challenge of Flow
3 To identify run-on sentence patterns (Complication) The "And-Then" Monster
  • T reads the "Run-on" example in Task 3 very fast, breathless.
  • Ask Ss: "Why was that hard to understand?" (Too long, no breathing).
  • Introduce the Complication: We try to say too much at once.
  • Ss rewrite the paragraph in Task 3, adding 3 full stops.
 10 S/Ss
4 To clarify sentence boundaries Feedback & Analysis
  • Check Task 3. Ask pairs to read their new sentences aloud.
  • Does it sound better? Clearer?
  • Concept Check: "Do we use 'also' to start a new idea or just to add to a long list?" (New idea = new sentence).
 5 T-Ss
STAGE THREE: Resolution - Mastery in Context
5 To prepare for the writing task (Resolution) Pre-writing / Planning
  • Draw attention to Task 4: Recommendation Email.
  • Brainstorm Thai attractions briefly (Grand Palace, Chiang Mai, etc.).
  • Remind Ss: It's only 70 words, but it MUST be perfect punctuation.
 5 T-Ss
6 To produce the final text Production
  • Ss write their emails alone.
  • Target: 5 clear sentences. No run-ons. Correct spacing.
  • T monitors and points silently at "floating dots" for Ss to self-correct.
 10 S
7 To verify mastery Peer Check / Resolution
  • Ss swap papers with a partner.
  • Use the checklist in Task 4.
  • If they find a floating dot, circle it. If they find a small 'thailand', circle it.
  • Return to owner to fix.
 3 Ss-Ss

Total Time: 46 minutes

Answer Key

Task 1 (Spacing): 1. Incorrect, 2. Correct, 3. Incorrect, 4. Correct, 5. Incorrect

Task 2 (Capitalisation): The, it, Thai, history, Also, Thai, Google, Translate.

Task 3 (Run-ons): Write what you gonna talk with them or ask any thing that you wana ask. Also dont be bad with them too because Thai people are friendly.
