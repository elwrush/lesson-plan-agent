Punctuation & Capitalisation Masterclass

Level: B1 | Topic: Writing Skills/Grammar | Time: 46 mins

1. The Space Invaders

The Rule: In English, punctuation ( . , ! ? ) "sticks" to the word before it. There is one space after it.

❌ Wrong: I went to the shop . It was closed .

✅ Right: I went to the shop. It was closed.

2. Captain Capital

The Rule: Use CAPITAL letters for:

  • At the start of a sentence (The...)
  • Names of people (John)
  • I (always!)
  • Countries, Nationalities, and Languages (Thailand, Thai, English)
  • Specific places and brands (Bangkok Hospital, Google, Facebook)

3. The "And Then" Monster

The Problem: Don't connect everything with because, and, or also. Stop. Breathe. Start a new sentence.

❌ Run-on: Stick to the rules because Thai people are friendly also to be sure be quiet to them too.

✅ Better: Stick to the rules because Thai people are friendly. Also, be sure to be quiet.

4. Writing Task: Recommendation

Task: Write a short email (70 words) to a foreign friend recommending a tourist attraction in Thailand.

The time that museum open is 10:00 am because it [lowercase] can help you find about Thai communication history [lowercase]. Also for tips to talk with Thai people just use Google Translate.

  • The (Start of sentence)
  • it (Not a proper noun, middle of sentence)
  • Thai (Nationality)
  • history (Common noun, unless specific title)
  • Also (Start of sentence)
  • Thai (Nationality)
  • Google Translate (Brand/Product name)

3. The "And Then" Monster

Suggested Answer (variations are possible):

Write what you are going to say to them or ask anything you want to ask. Also, don't be rude to them because Thai people are friendly.

(Or broken down further):

Write down what you want to talk about. You can also ask any questions you have. Don't be rude, because Thai people are friendly.
