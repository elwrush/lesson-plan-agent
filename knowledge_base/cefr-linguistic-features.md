# CEFR Linguistic Features for English Texts

This document provides a reference for distinguishing features of English texts at each CEFR level. Use this when generating differentiated reading texts or listening transcripts.

---

## A1 (Breakthrough)

### Grammar Structures
- **Verbs**: Present Simple (`I like`, `She works`), `to be`, `have got`
- **Questions**: Yes/No questions, Wh-questions (What, Where, Who)
- **Determiners**: a/an/the, this/that, my/your
- **Prepositions**: in, on, at, to, from (basic location/time)
- **Negation**: don't/doesn't, isn't/aren't

### Vocabulary
- **Range**: ~500-1000 high-frequency words
- **Type**: Concrete nouns (family, food, places), basic verbs (go, eat, like), simple adjectives (big, small, good)
- **Source**: Oxford 3000 (A1 band)

### Cohesion & Discourse
- **Connectors**: and, but, or, because
- **Sequencing**: First, then, next (very limited)
- **Reference**: Repetition of nouns (no pronoun chains)

### Text Features
- **Sentence Length**: 5-8 words average
- **Text Length**: 50-100 words
- **Structure**: Simple declarative sentences, isolated facts

---

## A2 (Waystage)

### Grammar Structures
- **Verbs**: Past Simple (regular/irregular), Future with `going to`, Present Continuous
- **Comparatives/Superlatives**: bigger, the biggest
- **Modals**: can, could (ability), must (obligation)
- **Infinitives**: want to, need to, like to
- **Adverbs of Frequency**: always, usually, sometimes, never

### Vocabulary
- **Range**: ~1000-2000 words
- **Type**: Expanded concrete nouns, action verbs, common phrasal verbs (get up, look for)
- **Topics**: Daily routines, shopping, travel, weather

### Cohesion & Discourse
- **Connectors**: and, but, so, because, when, before, after
- **Time Markers**: yesterday, last week, tomorrow, in the morning
- **Pronouns**: He/she/it/they for simple reference

### Text Features
- **Sentence Length**: 8-12 words average
- **Text Length**: 100-150 words
- **Structure**: Short paragraphs, chronological narratives, simple descriptions

---

## B1 (Threshold)

### Grammar Structures
- **Verbs**: Present Perfect Simple (`I have visited`), Past Continuous, used to
- **Modals**: should, might, may (advice, possibility)
- **Conditionals**: First Conditional (If + present, will + infinitive)
- **Passive Voice**: Simple present/past passive (`It was made`)
- **Relative Clauses**: who, which, that (defining)

### Vocabulary
- **Range**: ~2000-3500 words
- **Type**: Abstract nouns (experience, opportunity), opinion adjectives, common idioms
- **Collocations**: make a decision, have an effect

### Cohesion & Discourse
- **Connectors**: however, although, on the other hand, as a result
- **Discourse Markers**: In my opinion, For example, In fact
- **Pronoun Reference**: More complex chains, use of "this/that" for text reference

### Text Features
- **Sentence Length**: 12-18 words average
- **Text Length**: 150-250 words
- **Structure**: Multi-paragraph, clear topic sentences, basic argumentation

---

## B2 (Vantage)

### Grammar Structures
- **Verbs**: Present Perfect Continuous, Past Perfect, Future Perfect
- **Conditionals**: Second Conditional (If + past, would + infinitive), Third Conditional
- **Passive Voice**: All tenses, including continuous passive
- **Relative Clauses**: Non-defining clauses, reduced relatives
- **Reported Speech**: Full range including tense backshift
- **Inversion**: Not only...but also, Rarely..., Never...

### Vocabulary
- **Range**: ~3500-5000 words
- **Type**: Formal/informal register awareness, idiomatic expressions, academic vocabulary
- **Collocation**: Strong collocational awareness, less common phrasal verbs

### Cohesion & Discourse
- **Connectors**: Nevertheless, Furthermore, In contrast, Consequently, Despite
- **Hedging**: It seems that, tend to, It is likely that
- **Reference**: Complex anaphora, ellipsis, substitution

### Text Features
- **Sentence Length**: 18-25 words average
- **Text Length**: 250-400 words
- **Structure**: Complex argumentation, multiple perspectives, clear thesis statements

---

## C1 (Effective Operational Proficiency)

### Grammar Structures
- **Verbs**: Mixed conditionals, Future Perfect Continuous
- **Cleft Sentences**: It was John who..., What I need is...
- **Subjunctive**: It's essential that he be there
- **Advanced Passive**: Have something done, Get something done
- **Inversion**: Under no circumstances, Had I known...
- **Nominalization**: The destruction of..., His refusal to...

### Vocabulary
- **Range**: ~5000-8000 words
- **Type**: Low-frequency academic vocabulary, discipline-specific terms, nuanced synonyms
- **Register**: Full command of formal/informal switching

### Cohesion & Discourse
- **Connectors**: Notwithstanding, Insofar as, By the same token
- **Meta-discourse**: It is worth noting that, As previously mentioned
- **Thematic Progression**: Topic-comment structure, sophisticated paragraph linking

### Text Features
- **Sentence Length**: 20-30 words average
- **Text Length**: 400-600 words
- **Structure**: Sophisticated argumentation, implicit relationships, stance marking

---

## C2 (Mastery)

### Grammar Structures
- **Full Native Range**: All grammatical structures used with native-like accuracy and appropriacy
- **Stylistic Variation**: Deliberate choices for effect (e.g., sentence fragments, rhetorical questions)
- **Rare Structures**: Subjunctive in formal contexts, archaic forms for effect

### Vocabulary
- **Range**: 8000+ words (receptive: 10,000+)
- **Type**: Rare/archaic words, technical jargon, culturally-loaded expressions
- **Precision**: Fine-grained lexical distinctions

### Cohesion & Discourse
- **Full Range**: All devices used appropriately
- **Implicit Cohesion**: Logical relationships inferred rather than stated
- **Rhetorical Devices**: Irony, understatement, allusion

### Text Features
- **Sentence Length**: Variable (5-40+ words), deliberate rhythm
- **Text Length**: 500+ words
- **Structure**: Nuanced, layered argumentation; genre-appropriate conventions
