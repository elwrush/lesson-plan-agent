## Slide 17: Task 2 Instructions
- **Visual**: Standard Layout.
- **Text**: "Task 2: Synonym Match. Replace the underlined words."
- **Timer**: 5 Minutes.

## Slide 18: Answer 2.1 (Daily)
- **Visual**: Green Tint.
- **Text**: "The food is delivered **every day**." -> **DAILY**.
- **Why**: "Every day" means it happens daily.

## Slide 19: Answer 2.2 (Demand)
- **Visual**: Green Tint.
- **Text**: "There is a real **need**..." -> **DEMAND**.
- **Why**: When people want or need a product, there is a "demand" for it.

## Slide 20: Answer 2.3 (Aim)
- **Visual**: Green Tint.
- **Text**: "Our **plan** is to open..." -> **AIM**.
- **Why**: Your "aim" is your future goal or plan.

## Slide 21: Answer 2.4 (Firm)
- **Visual**: Green Tint.
- **Text**: "The **company** is doing well." -> **FIRM**.
- **Why**: "Firm" is another common word for a business or company.

## Slide 22: Answer 2.5 (Employees)
- **Visual**: Green Tint.
- **Text**: "We have 25 **workers**." -> **EMPLOYEES**.
- **Why**: People who work for a company are its employees.

## Slide 23: Answer 2.6 (Set Up)
- **Visual**: Green Tint.
- **Text**: "I **started** the business..." -> **SET UP**.
- **Why**: To "set up" a business means to start or create it.

## Slide 24: Answer 2.7 (Headquarters)
- **Visual**: Green Tint.
- **Text**: "Their **main offices**..." -> **HEADQUARTERS**.
- **Why**: The most important office of a company is its headquarters (HQ).

## Slide 25: Answer 2.8 (Position)
- **Visual**: Green Tint.
- **Text**: "He's hoping for a **job**..." -> **POSITION**.
- **Why**: In business, a specific job title is often called a "position".

## Slide 26: Answer 2.9 (Expanding)
- **Visual**: Green Tint.
- **Text**: "The firm is **getting bigger**." -> **EXPANDING**.
- **Why**: To "expand" means to grow larger or open new branches.

## Slide 27: Answer 2.10 (Great Deal)
- **Visual**: Green Tint.
- **Text**: "The company made **a lot** of money." -> **A GREAT DEAL**.
- **Why**: "A great deal" is a formal way to say "a lot" or "a large amount".

## Slide 29: Task 3 Instructions
- **Visual**: Standard Layout.
- **Text**: "Task 3: Choose the best word to complete the sentences."
- **Timer**: 4 Minutes.

## Slide 30: Answer 3.1 (Experts)
- **Visual**: Green Tint.
- **Text**: "They're experts **in** farming."
- **Why**: We always say "expert IN" a subject or field.

## Slide 31: Answer 3.2 (Take Up)
- **Visual**: Green Tint.
- **Text**: "When did you **take up** tennis?"
- **Why**: You "take up" a hobby (start it). You "set up" a business.

## Slide 32: Answer 3.3 (Clients)
- **Visual**: Green Tint.
- **Text**: "Her law firm has many famous **clients**."
- **Why**: Professional services (lawyers, accountants) have clients. Shops have customers.

## Slide 33: Answer 3.4 (Ex-Wife)
- **Visual**: Green Tint.
- **Text**: "She's actually my **ex-**wife."
- **Why**: We use "Ex-" for relationships (ex-wife, ex-boyfriend). We use "Former" for job titles.

## Slide 34: Answer 3.5 (Set Up)
- **Visual**: Green Tint.
- **Text**: "When did they **set up** the company?"
- **Why**: To "set up" means to build or start a company.

## Slide 35: Answer 3.6 (Customer)
- **Visual**: Green Tint.
- **Text**: "The shop assistant was serving a **customer**."
- **Why**: In a shop, the person buying things is a "customer".

## Slide 36: Answer 3.7 (Currently)
- **Visual**: Green Tint.
- **Text**: "He's **currently** working abroad."
- **Why**: "Currently" means "right now". "Actually" means "in fact" or "surprisingly".

## Slide 37: Answer 3.8 (Take Over)
- **Visual**: Green Tint.
- **Text**: "Are they planning to **take over** the company?"
- **Why**: To "take over" means to buy control of another company.

## Slide 38: Answer 3.9 (Former)
- **Visual**: Green Tint.
- **Text**: "George Bush is a **former** president."
- **Why**: We use "Former" for official job titles in the past.

## Slide 40: Task 4 Instructions
- **Visual**: Standard Layout.
- **Text**: "Rewrite the sentences using the word in CAPITALS."
- **Timer**: 6 Minutes.

## Slide 41: Answer 4.1 (Variety)
- **Visual**: Green Tint. Auto-Animate.
- **Before**: "He makes different cakes."
- **After**: "He makes a **variety** of cakes."
- **Why**: "Variety" (Noun) is the correct word for "many different types".

## Slide 42: Answer 4.2 (Achieved)
- **Visual**: Green Tint. Auto-Animate.
- **Before**: "They were very successful."
- **After**: "They **achieved** a great deal."
- **Why**: To "achieve" success means to reach your goal.

## Slide 43: Answer 4.3 (Contacts)
- **Visual**: Green Tint. Auto-Animate.
- **Before**: "I know a lot of people in banking."
- **After**: "I have many **contacts** in banking."
- **Why**: "Contacts" are useful people you know in business.

## Slide 44: Answer 4.4 (Formerly)
- **Visual**: Green Tint. Auto-Animate.
- **Before**: "They used to be called BMG."
- **After**: "They were **formerly** known as BMG."
- **Why**: "Formerly" is a formal word meaning "in the past".

## Slide 45: Answer 4.5 (Ambition)
- **Visual**: Green Tint. Auto-Animate.
- **Before**: "I've always wanted to fly a plane."
- **After**: "It's my **ambition** to fly a plane."
- **Why**: Your "ambition" is your strong desire to do something in the future.

## Slide 46: Answer 4.6 (Expert)
- **Visual**: Green Tint. Auto-Animate.
- **Before**: "She has a great knowledge of finance."
- **After**: "She is an **expert** in finance."
- **Why**: An "expert" is a person who knows a lot about a specific subject.

## Slide 47: Answer 4.7 (Attention)
- **Visual**: Green Tint. Auto-Animate.
- **Before**: "People noticed the adverts."
- **After**: "The adverts attracted a lot of **attention**."
- **Why**: To "attract attention" means to make people look or notice.
